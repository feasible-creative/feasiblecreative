4 MUST-KNOW Midjourney Parameters to Make Mind-Blowing AI Art

Nov 23, 2022

Midjourney Explained

Midjourney is a San Francisco-based research lab that has developed an AI Art Generator currently operating through a Discord server. Users can create mind-blowing art images in seconds by typing text-based commands into the chat that instruct the artificial intelligence engine to run. At first glance, the idea of developing these commands can seem overwhelming. For this reason, in this article we cover 4 Essential Parameters you can use to start creating incredible AI Art with Midjourney!

1. --ar (Aspect Ratio)

This parameter allows users to manipulate the aspect ratio of images generated by Midjourney. The default aspect ratio is 1:1 (1 by 1) which is essentially a standard square shape. As you continue on your AI Art journey, you will likely want to adjust the dimensions of your designs.

Midjourney supports many other aspect ratios beyond 1:1, including 16:9 which is the standard for widescreen TV and computer screens. This parameter can be added to your commands by typing in "--ar [desired aspect ratio]" For example, "--ar 3:2" (without quotation marks) will output images generated in the 3:2 aspect ratio. 2. 4K, 8K, Intricate Details, Ultra Realistic (Resolution Keywords)

Resolution keywords including "4K" and "Ultra Realistic" can be added to your Midjourney commands to adjust the detail level of your images. You likely noticed the Mona Lisa design above lacks realistic details, which is a result of not including any resolution keywords. These resolution keywords can come especially in handy when you are looking to generate a specific style of art.

The following image was created by including the "8K" keyword:

Clearly, this image is much more detailed when compared to the default images the Midjourney engine outputs.

3. --chaos (Abstraction Level)

As the name suggests, the "chaos" parameter can be added to your Midjourney commands to adjust the level of Abstraction. By typing in "--chaos [Level of Abstraction from 0 - 100]" you can utilize the chaos parameter. For example, including "--chaos 50" within your command will output images with a medium level of abstraction. The following image does an excellent job of comparing the difference between the minimum and maximum levels of chaos (0, 100 respectively):

4. --no (Remove/Withhold Specific Keyword)

Let's say you want to create soup without broth, or a zoo without animals - in these instances, using the "no" parameter is very helpful. The syntax is "--no [whatever subject you would like to be withheld] (ex: "A futuristic zoo, --no animals")

In the below image, the Midjourney command included "--no seats" which resulted in a person boarding a train without any seats available.
